\textbf{For each problem, sketch a timeline, write an equation in one variable, and 
        solve for that variable to answer the question.}

\vspace{.2in}

\begin{questions}

    \question Five (5) years ago, you put \$100 in a savings account earning 3.5\% 
            interest.  How much do you have in the account now?
    \vspace{2in}

    \question You just lent a friend \$300 and told him you'd like him to repay you 
            someday at 7\% annual interest.  How much does he owe you if he pays 
            you back two years from now?
    \vspace{2in}

    \question Same situation as in problem 2, but your friend pays you back in only 
            6 months.  How much does he owe you then?
    \vspace{2in}

    \question You buy shares in a mutual fund for \$150.  After 6 years, you sell your
            shares for \$212.78. What annual rate of interest did you earn?
    \vspace{2in}

    \question You buy \$1,000 worth of stock in a company whose stock price is growing at
            a rate of 10\% per year.  How many years must you wait before the stock is worth
            \$1,250?
    \vspace{2in}

    \question At 5\% annual interest, how long does it take for your money to triple?
    \vspace{2in}





\end{questions}
